What the tweets of America's most iconic presidents tell us about their leadership

Throughout history, the mediums by which American presidents addressed their nation continued to change over time.

Newspaper publications, radio broadcasts and television programmes were some of the ways that national messages were communicated over space and time.

In social media, powerful leaders, particularly in politics have found a new platform to keep the people up to date with their policies and at all hours of the day.

In his innovative use of social media, Obama earned the title of the first social media president, but during his time in office, Trump surely earned the title of the social media president.

Despite their similarities in using social media, both could not be any more different.

Born of a white Kansas mother and black African American father, Obama became a symbol of unity when he entered office in 2008. It wasn't just his background, but also his policies and unifying approach to leading a diversifying nation that drove his identity.

On the other hand, Trump entered the race to the White House as an unpopular contender, largely for his divisive rhetoric wielded against the Hispanic and Muslim communities. Carrying out unpopular policies such as 'Building the Wall' to keep illegal immigrants out and withdrawing from global climate agreements were thought to isolate the US from the world, and the people within it, from each toher.

How does Twitter come into this? Obama and Trump were the first presidents to utilise social media not only to communicate ongoing policies, but also to comment on happening events.

It is a mouthpiece by which people have been able to communicate their thoughts about current events within 140 characters or less.

This visualisation seeks to explore how their language on the social media platform indicates what kind of leader they are.

What is leadership?

However, Obama's approach to leadership couldn't be any more different than Trump. With a Unite and Build approach, Obama sought to bring diverse groups together to build a united nation.

We can identify this from their language.

Political correctness begins with a belief in using inclusive language—words and phrases that have not historically been used to demean, belittle, or insult any particular group of people.

Although it is a term that has been around for centuries, Political Correctness has grown to be a hot button topic. This is particularly true in the United States, arguably the most powerful nation in the world which continues to grow both demographically and economically.

Political correctness has also been exacerbated, social media platforms like Twitter, have become a breeding ground for criticisms and exchanged words against political correctness.
